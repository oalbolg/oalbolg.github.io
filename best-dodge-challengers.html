Best Dodge Challengers (2008–2023 Models Guide)

Published | May 24, 2026

The 2008–2023 Dodge Challenger lineup represents one of the most powerful and visually iconic eras in modern American muscle car history. Reviving the classic nameplate with a bold retro-inspired design and modern engineering, these newer Challengers blend old-school muscle attitude with today’s performance technology, comfort, and safety features. From daily-driving V6 trims to supercharged Hellcat monsters, this generation has something for nearly every type of performance enthusiast.

In this guide, we’ll break down the best Dodge Challengers, cool Dodge Challengers, popular Dodge Challengers, fast Dodge Challengers, and new Dodge Challengers from the 2008–2023 model years, focusing only on this modern era—not the classic 1970s versions.

The Modern Era of Dodge Challenger (2008–2023)

When Dodge relaunched the Challenger in 2008, it instantly stood out in a crowded performance market. Unlike many competitors that moved toward smaller, turbocharged engines or sports coupe platforms, the Challenger stayed true to its muscle car roots: rear-wheel drive, aggressive styling, and big displacement engines.

Over the years, Dodge refined the platform while expanding performance options. By the 2015 refresh and especially the Hellcat introduction, the Challenger became one of the most powerful production muscle cars in the world.

This generation includes several key trims:

  • Challenger SE / SXT (V6 entry-level models)
  • Challenger R/T (5.7L HEMI V8)
  • Challenger Scat Pack (6.4L 392 HEMI V8)
  • Challenger SRT Hellcat (supercharged 6.2L V8)
  • Challenger SRT Demon / Super Stock (drag-focused performance variants)

Best Dodge Challengers (2008–2023)

When it comes to the best Dodge Challengers, the answer depends on what you value most: balance, performance, or collectability.

The Challenger R/T Scat Pack is widely considered one of the best all-around options. With its 6.4L HEMI V8 producing over 485 horsepower, it delivers serious straight-line speed while still being usable as a daily driver. It offers a perfect middle ground between affordability and extreme performance.

For those who want the ultimate experience, the SRT Hellcat Redeye stands out as one of the best high-performance trims ever produced in this era. With over 797 horsepower, it transformed the Challenger into a supercharged street beast capable of competing with exotic sports cars in a straight line.

Meanwhile, collectors often favor limited editions like the Demon (2018), which was designed specifically for drag racing and became one of the most extreme factory muscle cars ever built.

Cool Dodge Challengers (Styling and Presence)

The cool Dodge Challengers from 2008–2023 aren’t just about raw horsepower—they’re about presence. This car’s retro-inspired wide stance, long hood, and aggressive grille make it one of the most recognizable vehicles on the road.

Special appearance packages like the Shaker Hood editions and T/A packages add vintage-inspired styling cues while enhancing airflow and performance aesthetics. Matte paint options, widebody kits introduced later in the generation, and bold color choices such as Go Mango, Plum Crazy Purple, and Hellraisin further cement the Challenger’s reputation as a style icon.

Popular Dodge Challengers (Fan Favorites)

Among enthusiasts and everyday drivers, the popular Dodge Challengers tend to fall into three main categories: affordability, power, and reliability.

The SXT and GT models are extremely popular due to their lower price point, comfortable ride, and surprisingly strong V6 engine producing up to 303 horsepower. These models made Challenger ownership accessible to a wider audience without sacrificing style.

The R/T and Scat Pack models are also fan favorites because they deliver authentic V8 muscle car performance without reaching extreme price levels. Many buyers choose these trims as a “sweet spot” for performance and cost.

The Hellcat lineup remains highly popular among performance enthusiasts who want supercar-level horsepower in a traditional American muscle car package.

Fast Dodge Challengers (Performance Kings)

When discussing the fast Dodge Challengers, the Hellcat variants dominate the conversation.

The standard SRT Hellcat produces 707–717 horsepower depending on the model year, capable of accelerating from 0–60 mph in around 3.6 seconds. This level of performance was groundbreaking for a four-seat coupe.

The Hellcat Redeye takes things even further, pushing close to 797 horsepower and improving acceleration and quarter-mile performance.

At the top of the performance hierarchy, the Dodge Challenger SRT Demon (2018) is purpose-built for drag racing. It can run the quarter mile in under 10 seconds under ideal conditions.

New Dodge Challengers (Final Years: 2020–2023)

The new Dodge Challengers from the final production years (2020–2023) represent the peak evolution of this platform. Dodge refined technology, expanded widebody availability, and introduced more special editions to celebrate the model’s legacy.

The widebody versions of the Scat Pack and Hellcat became especially popular, improving handling and giving the Challenger a more aggressive, track-ready stance.

By 2023, Dodge began transitioning the Challenger lineup toward its next phase of electrified performance, marking the end of an era for the classic gas-powered muscle coupe.

Final Thoughts

The 2008–2023 Dodge Challenger lineup stands as one of the most complete modern muscle car families ever built. Whether you're looking for the best Dodge Challengers, admiring the cool Dodge Challengers, exploring popular Dodge Challengers, chasing the fast Dodge Challengers, or shopping for new Dodge Challengers, this generation delivers something unforgettable at every level.

It successfully bridges the gap between classic American muscle heritage and modern performance engineering, ensuring its place as a long-lasting icon in automotive history.
